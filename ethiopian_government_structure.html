About Ethiopia's Government

Ethiopia operates under a federal parliamentary republic system with ethnic federalism. The Constitution (1995) establishes three branches of government at the federal level, with significant powers devolved to 12 regional states and 2 chartered cities.

📜 Federal Parliamentary Assembly

Ethiopia's bicameral parliament creates laws and manages the federal structure. The HoPR holds primary legislative authority, while the HoF ensures ethnic representation and constitutional interpretation.

🏛️
House of Peoples' Representatives (HoPR)

The Lower House

• Size: Up to 550 members

• Term: 5 years

• Mandate: Sole federal legislative body

Powers & Functions:

Pass National Laws Approve Federal Budget Appoint Prime Minister Confirm Federal Judges Ratify Treaties Declare State of Emergency
🤝
House of Federation (HoF)

The Upper House

• Composition: Ethnic group representatives

• Minimum: 1 rep per ethnic group + population-based extras

• Unique Role: Does NOT draft standard legislation

Special Mandates:

Interpret Constitution Resolve Inter-State Disputes Revenue-Sharing Formula Federal Block Grants State Boundary Issues

💰 Constitutional Division of Taxation Powers

The Ethiopian Constitution (Articles 96–99) explicitly divides taxation powers into four categories, ensuring both federal and regional governments have specific revenue streams for fiscal autonomy.

96
Federal Power of Taxation
Article 96 — Exclusive Federal Taxes
Federal Share of Total Revenue~45%
  • Customs duties, import surtaxes and taxes on imports/exports
  • Income tax on federal government employees
  • Taxes on federal state-owned enterprises
  • Taxes on air, rail, and sea transport services
  • National lottery taxes
  • Federal property income and stamp duties
Collector: Ministry of Revenues & Ethiopian Customs Commission
97
State (Regional) Power
Article 97 — Exclusive Regional Taxes
Regional Share of Own Revenue~30%
  • Income taxes on regional state employees and private employees within the state
  • Fees for land usufructuary rights (land lease fees)
  • Agricultural income taxes from private farmers and cooperatives
  • Profit and sales taxes on individual traders within the state
  • Taxes on income from private properties
Collector: Regional Revenue Authorities
98
Concurrent Power of Taxation
Article 98 — Shared Federal/Regional
Split 50/50 (Recent Reform)50%
  • Taxes on jointly established enterprises: Profit, sales, excise, and personal income
  • Private corporate company taxes — recent reforms: 50/50 split
  • Taxes and royalties from mining, petroleum, and gas operations
Revenue Sharing: Divided per HoF-determined formula
99
Undesignated Power
Article 99 — New/Unspecified Taxes
Requires 2/3 Joint Vote67%
  • For any new or unspecified taxes (e.g., modern property taxes, motor vehicle taxes)
  • Requires two-thirds majority in joint session of HoPR and HoF
  • Assigns taxing authority to either federal or regional level by joint decision
Process: Joint parliamentary decision required
